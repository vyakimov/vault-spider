# Brothers Karamazov — Name Glossary

[[book notes]] — who's who in *The Brothers Karamazov* (Russian names are a lot), through
Book 2. Companion to [[Brothers Karamazov — Summary]].

Spoiler-safe through **Book 2**.

## Fyodor Pavlovich Karamazov
The father. Buffoonish, sensual, grasping landowner; twice widowed. In open conflict with Dmitri
over money and, soon, over a woman.

## Dmitri Fyodorovich Karamazov / Mitya / Mitenka
Eldest son, from the first marriage. An ex-officer: passionate, impulsive, generous and
dissolute at once. Believes his father withheld his maternal inheritance.

## Ivan Fyodorovich Karamazov / Vanya
Middle son, first son of the second marriage. Cold, brilliant, essayist; his article on church
courts is debated in Zosima's cell.

## Alexei Fyodorovich Karamazov / Alyosha / Alyoshka
Youngest son. Gentle, devout novice at the monastery, Zosima's favorite. The narrator calls him
the hero of the book.

## Zosima
The elder (starets) at the monastery: aged, revered, dying. Counsels Fyodor to stop lying to
himself; receives the grieving peasant women.

## Grigory Vasilievich Kutuzov
Fyodor's old servant, rigid and loyal; raised the neglected boys when their father wouldn't.

## Smerdyakov / Pavel Fyodorovich Smerdyakov
Fyodor's cook and lackey; rumored son of Fyodor by Lizaveta Smerdyashchaya. Epileptic, sullen,
contemptuous.

## Lizaveta Smerdyashchaya
"Stinking Lizaveta" — a mute, homeless holy fool who died giving birth to Smerdyakov in Fyodor's
garden. The town rumor names Fyodor the father. She appears only in backstory.

## Liza Khokhlakova / Lise
Madame Khokhlakova's daughter: a teenage invalid in a bath-chair, sharp-tongued, teases Alyosha,
to whom she wrote a love letter. Not to be confused with Lizaveta Smerdyashchaya.

## Madame Khokhlakova / Katerina Osipovna Khokhlakova
A wealthy, excitable widow; Liza's mother; a chatterbox devoted to Zosima.

## Pyotr Alexandrovich Miusov
A cousin of Dmitri's mother: wealthy, liberal, vain; embarrassed by Fyodor at the monastery.

## Rakitin / Mikhail Osipovich Rakitin
A young seminarian, ambitious and cynical; Alyosha's ostensible friend.
