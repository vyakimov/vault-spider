# Brothers Karamazov — Themes

[[book notes]] — running list of themes while reading, sibling to
[[Brothers Karamazov — Summary]]. Thin so far; through Book 2 only.

- **"If there is no immortality, everything is permitted"** — planted as a debating point in
  the elder's cell long before anyone acts on it.
- **Active love vs. abstract love** — loving humanity in general is easy; the person in front
  of you is the hard part. Zosima keeps returning to this.
- **Buffoonery as armor** — shamelessness performed loudly enough becomes a defense against
  being seen.
- **Fathers** — biological, adoptive, spiritual. The novel keeps asking which one counts.

To revisit once further in: whether the narrator's irony is affection or indictment.
