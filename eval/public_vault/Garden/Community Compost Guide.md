# Community Compost Guide

## Basic mix

Build the pile with roughly three buckets of carbon-rich material for each bucket of nitrogen-rich
material. Dry leaves, shredded plain cardboard, and straw are “browns”; fresh plant trimmings,
vegetable scraps, and used coffee grounds are “greens”.

## Moisture and air

The pile should feel like a wrung-out sponge. Turn it when the centre cools or develops a sour
smell. A healthy active pile becomes warm within several days but does not need to reach a precise
temperature to make useful compost.

## What to exclude

Do not add treated wood, painted material, plastic, pet waste, meat, or oily food. Finished compost
smells earthy and no longer resembles the original ingredients.
